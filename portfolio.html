About Me

I’m an undergraduate Computer Science student at Lancaster University Leipzig, originally from Japan. I am exploring the interest still, but the intersection between technology and human-centered design. I’m especially interested in how users move through interfaces and how small design decisions impact clarity and comfort.

I don’t see myself as a “typical programmer” – I’m more interested in how things feel and how people actually use them.

I learned the basics of UI/UX design principles. My CS background has given me exposure to topics such as Algorithms, AI, neural networks, and basic network systems, but I’m especially drawn to work where I can connect technology with clear, gentle user experiences.

I speak Japanese (native) and English (around C1 level).
Outside of screens, I like photographing oceans, lakes, towns, and night skies, and spending time with my dogs. I’m interested in internships or junior roles where I can grow my design and product sense while contributing to real teams.
